Setup & Troubleshooting

Powering On

Simply plug in the USB-C cable. WellRound will power on automatically.

Entering Setup Mode

When first turning on, go to your Wi-Fi settings and connect to the network shown on WellRound’s screen.

If the WellRound name doesn't appear in Wi-Fi list, re-open your Wi-Fi list.

Before connecting, ensure you have your home Wi-Fi password. Once connected, a pairing portal appears.

Fill out the form and enter your location as City, Country Code. Ensure there's a comma that separates city and country (e.g. Dallas, US).

Finishing Setup - Device Did Not Reset

After submitting your info, WellRound should restart automatically. If it does not, credentials entereed are likely incorrect. Repeat the setup process.

Fixing “Failed to Get Time”

If you encounter this error after setup, unplug and plug the device back in. This typically resolves the issue.

Re-Entering Setup Mode

To reconfigure settings (Wi-Fi, location, number of reminders per hour etc.):
1. Power off the device by unplugging the USB-C.
2. When the “KH” logo appears during reboot, press and hold the left button for 2–3 seconds.
3. Release to re-enter setup mode. Follow "Entering Setup Mode" instructions above for help.

Device Usage

Clock Mode

This is the default screen. The number above the right arrow shows the current day date.

Left button will take you to WeatherView.

Middle button will to toggle between 12/24-hour formats.

Right button will take you to FocusTimer screen

WeatherView

From the clock screen, press the left button to view the weather. The display auto-refreshes based on your setup location.
Weather will display momentarily then take you back to Clock Mode.

FocusTimer

From the clock screen, press the right button to access FocusTimer.

Right button will take you back to clock mode.

Middle Button will increase time.

Left button will start/pause/clear the timer.

Health Reminders

On the clock screen, reminders (e.g., hydration, movement) will display based on your setup preferences. They pause at midnight and resume in the early morning.

Display Behavior

At midnight, the screen dims for better sleep. Around sunset, it shifts clock color to red to reduce blue light and protect your eyes.
