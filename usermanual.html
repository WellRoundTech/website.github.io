Setup & Troubleshooting

Powering On

Simply plug in the USB-C cable. DeskOrbie will power on automatically.

Entering Setup Mode

When first turning on, go to your Wi-Fi settings and connect to the network shown on DeskOrbie’s screen.

If the DeskOrbie name doesn't appear in the Wi-Fi list, re-open your Wi-Fi list.

Before connecting, ensure you have your home Wi-Fi password. Once connected, a pairing portal appears.

Fill out the form and enter your location as City, Country Code (e.g. Dallas, US).

Finishing Setup - Device Did Not Reset

After submitting your info, DeskOrbie should restart automatically. If it does not, the credentials entered are likely incorrect. Repeat the setup process.

Fixing “Failed to Get Time”

If you encounter this error after setup, unplug and plug the device back in. This typically resolves the issue.

Re-Entering Setup Mode

To reconfigure settings (Wi-Fi, location, number of reminders per hour, etc.):
1. Power off the device by unplugging the USB-C.
2. When the DeskOrbie logo appears during reboot, press and hold the left button for 2–3 seconds.
3. Release to re-enter setup mode. Then, follow the "Entering Setup Mode" instructions above.

Device Usage

Clock Mode

This is the default screen. The number above the right arrow shows the current day’s date.

Left button: Takes you to WeatherView.

Middle button: Toggles between 12/24-hour formats.

Right button: Takes you to the FocusTimer screen.

WeatherView

From the clock screen, press the left button to view the weather. The display auto-refreshes based on your setup location and then returns to Clock Mode.

FocusTimer

From the clock screen, press the right button to access FocusTimer.

Right button: Returns you to Clock Mode.

Middle button: Increases the time.

Left button: Starts/pauses/clears the timer.

Health Reminders

On the clock screen, health reminders (e.g., hydration, movement) will display based on your setup preferences. They pause at midnight and resume in the early morning.

Display Behavior

At midnight, the screen dims for better sleep. Around sunset, it shifts the clock color to red to reduce blue light and protect your eyes.
